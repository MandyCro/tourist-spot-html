Discover 3 must-see destinations in Busan

Busan's attractions range from centuries-old Buddhist temples dotting the city's mountains and coastline to pristine beaches with crystal-clear waters. This coastal city has plenty to do all year round – families can spend time at a seaside aquarium, shoppers can explore vibrant neighborhoods, and nature lovers can enjoy long hikes to panoramic viewpoints. The Buddhist sanctuaries dotting Busan's coast and mountains feature impressive architecture that will delight photographers.

1. Haedong Yonggungsa Temple

The Haedong Yonggungsa Temple is a Buddhist temple located in the northeastern tip of Busan. Built in 1376, it is one of the few temples in Korea built by the seaside – you can enjoy views of the East Sea on one side and beautiful mountains on the other.


Good for:

  • History

2. Beomeo-sa Temple

The Beomeo-sa Temple is one of the largest sanctuaries in South Korea. It is located high on the eastern edge of Mount Geumjeongsa and is far from the hustle and bustle of the city. The temple's Daeungjeon Hall is a well-preserved example of Joseon Dynasty architecture.


Good for:

  • History

3. Yongdusan Park

Yongdusan Park, located in downtown Busan, is home to some of the city's most important monuments. You can see spectacular views from the top of the 120-meter-high Busan Tower. The park has 2 museums – check out the traditional musical instruments at the Museum of World Folk Instruments and over 80 Korean sailboats at the Exhibition Hall of World Model Boats.


Good for:

  • Couples
  • Families
  • Budget
